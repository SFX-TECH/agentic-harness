Not every lesson is worth a permanent entry, and a bank full of one-off observations is noise. The promotion gate keeps the signal high: record on first sight, promote on recurrence.

## The gate

1. **Is this the first time you have seen this pattern?** If yes, record it as a dated CANDIDATE: a short name, what happened, and why it might generalize. Park it where candidates live (a candidates section, or a note in active-context.md). Do not give it a permanent number yet. One data point is a coincidence.

2. **Is this a recurrence of something already recorded as a candidate?** Then it has earned promotion. Append it to decisions.md as a numbered, dated operational observation: the rule in one line, the two or more instances that prove it, and how to apply it going forward. Now it is load-bearing knowledge a future session can rely on.

3. **Is it a one-off with no plausible reuse?** Let it go. The progress log already records what happened. Not everything needs to be a principle.

## Why the gate matters

A pattern promoted on a single instance is usually overfit to that instance. A pattern that recurs has shown it generalizes. Waiting for the second instance is the cheapest filter between a real operating principle and a premature rule. Verify the recurrence against the real candidate record before promoting, so you are matching an actual prior instance and not a half-remembered one.
